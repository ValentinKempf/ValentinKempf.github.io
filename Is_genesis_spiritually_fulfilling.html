Is genesis creation spiritually fulfilling?

Many creationists claim that evolution is nihilistic and that they derive a sense of purpose from their creation accounts, let's take a closer look at what evolution has to say and what genesis, interpreted literally, has to say and on the way let's compare whose model has more spiritual significance attached to it.

Evolution describes the origin of life as we know it today as the growth of a literal family tree, in which every single branch has reached its present state through 4 billion years of autonomous self-directed development. Every species on earth is related to every other according to evolution. In Genesis the story is completely different. In Genesis, the origin of life was confined to less than a week, and everything was created by some dictator in heaven who plonked all living creatures into the world, isolated, without relationships and without kinship.

From this we see that there are three aspects in which evolution holds more spiritual significance: the relatedness of life, the length of creation and the autonomy of development. It is astounding to me that I have never heard such a comparison laid out in this clarity before, even though it’s very easy to grasp. It’s even more astounding that many people seem to reverse reality in claiming that evolution is soulless, random and nihilistic, while the religious view is somehow awe-inspiring and ascribes an inherent purpose to the biosphere.

That's the thrust of the argument, but the more you look into the details, the starker this picture this becomes, so there are other factors driving the evolutionary account to be more spiritually fulfilling. For instance the capacity for change: evolution describes the process of continuous change, whose creative power has birthed a historical diversity, which leaves modern biodiversity in the dust. Just think of all the dinosaurs, the mammals, massive prehistoric sharks and so on. All of this is missing in the accounts of Genesis, whose creation will eternally be damned to a petrified static stagnancy. And how big will be the diversity of things yet to come in evolution, all of which is denied by an account of creation!

Next comes the concept of equality. In evolutionary terms, all living beings are equal in terms of their ancestry; all have emerged through 4 billion years of evolution. This does not, of course, mean that they are equal in terms of body size, habitat, diversity or intelligence, but there is no inherent distinction between ‘lower animals’ and ‘higher animals’, all have undergone a similar process of 4 billion years of evolution; none have been left behind with a ‘lower’ level of development. By comparison, Genesis presents a grotesque division between higher and lower animals: ‘the complex creatures’, the the crown of creation as compared to the simple earthworms that crawl through the dirt. Admittedly, there are religious people who regard this ‘ladder of complexity’ (scala naturae) as meaningful, but that is solely because their priest has talked them into it. In reality, it merely serves to demean other animals or, to put it in traditional Christian terms: ‘that the beasts are worthless in comparison to humans’ [1] If this doctrine has any effect on our view of humanity, it is surely to isolate us in the belief that we are the only sentient beings. How impoverished our view of humanity seems when we cannot engage in a lively exchange with chimpanzees, orangutans, gorillas and the rest of the magnificent monkeykind and apekind.

In addition, we can remark about the primitiveness of the biblical world-view, for example: people have thought that all land animals would fit on a single boat. According to this we can conclude how the old jews assumed, that there might be a few hundred species of animals, when in reality there exist 8 million species of eukaryotes alone, not to mention all of the prokaryotes and viruses. Noah's arch is obviously impossible with our modern knowledge of biodiversity; the only ship, which is big enough to fit all the wonders of evolution is earth itself, and that only with considerable effort. And even then it is at the risk of sinking under the weight of 10 billion people.

The second subpoint concerning the primitiveness of the biblical world-view is the attempt at an explanation . Evolution is capable of giving very specific explanations about the distribution of traits among animals and plants. For instance our spine is explained by common descent or homology with other vertebrates and especially other mammals and primates. So one need not wonder, why the same types of body parts are shared by so many animals: eyes, teeth, bones, four limbs in mammals. Eight legs, spinneret, lungs and fangs in spiders and so on. Common descent explains, why these traits are shared by many animals. On the other hand evolution also explains the differences between species, namely by adaptation: what is the specific way of life of this organism, and what changes to its homologous body parts have to be made because of it? Adaptations often result in convergent evolution or analogy; bat wings, bird wings and insect wings all evolved independently of one another, yet they all serve the same function: flight. Therefore we say that the wings of birds, bats and insects are analogous . In the case of bats and birds their wings are also homologous , because they are both modified forelimbs, but the wings of insects are not homologous .

Therefore evolution can specifically explain all the characteristics of every living being on Earth through a combination of homology and adaptation, and in many specific cases also through analogy. Let us now compare this with Genesis. Is there any explanation as to why living beings are the way they are and not different? Why don’t crocodiles have wings like birds? God made them that way. Why do a chimpanzee’s face and body look so similar to those of a human? God made them that way. That is the universal explanation: no matter which living creature one examines, no matter which characteristic one wishes to explain, the explanation is always ‘God made it that way’; and if the world were different – if dinosaurs still existed and if horses had a horn on their foreheads – then one would have to say: ‘God made it differently’. But an explanation that can explain everything essentially explains nothing, as the explanation would be unfalsifiable.

Evolution, precisely because it explains so much, is also easy to falsify. The only thing you'd have to do is find an animal or plant, which has no spot in the tree of life. If you could only find a strange mutant, with the head of a crocodile, the hooves of a horse, wings of a bat and eyes of a fly, then we would be stumped and we could find no place for it in the tree of life. But this has never happened and will never happen, every organism is very easy to place into its respective category. Every new animal we discover can easily be placed in the tree of life, it is either a ray finned fish or a beetle, or a lizard, or a primate or something else, we never find unexpected mixtures of these creatures. The creationist would have no problem explaining such a mutant ‘God just made it that way’, but precisely because of this his theory is not falsifiable und therefore explains nothing [2].

So it has been shown that creationism is not only acidic to science (as is well known among people educated in biology), but also anti-spiritual and unfulfilling as a world-view. So even if both accounts were equally plausible scientifically, we could still prefer evolution on spiritual grounds alone.

Of course most religionists accept evolution and don't think that god was particularly involved in the creation of life, or they might say that god was „guiding evolution along its way“, whatever that means. While I personally prefer the completely impersonal account of the origins and development of life, I can't say that these more mainstream religious beliefs are anti-scientific or anti-spiritual [3].Thus any non-creationists christians should not get triggered, for I do not intend to denigrate their world-view. If you're a christian you shouldn't hesitate to call out people abusing your scripture for anti-scientific and anti-spiritual purposes (e.g. creationism).

[3] That said, at least one of the eight arguments remains valid for theistic evolution, namely that of autonomous development. The impersonal theory of evolution states that natural selection and undirected mutation have led to today’s life forms; these are factors inherent in life, and life develops according to its own laws—that is, autonomously. “Auto” means “self” and “nomos” means “law”; therefore, “autonomous” means “according to its own laws.” Theistic evolution, on the other hand, also requires aspects of directed mutation or divine selection, or a combination of both. These are factors outside of life that influence its development. In this sense, we can refer to this as heteronomous development (according to the laws of another). Despite all this, this is the weakest of the eight arguments presented, since many people have no quibbles to a heteronomous explanation of creation, especially if it is guided by a deity presumed to be benevolent.
